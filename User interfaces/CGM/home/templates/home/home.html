METHODOLOGY

Our work process, assets set and financial models

Work Process

CGM aims to deliver an automated investment management services (also called Robo-advisor) to maximize the long-term returns. We choose low-expense passive ETFs that stand for many different indices from various capital market to diversify your investment. We use Black-Litterman as our base model and use Modern Portfolio Theory to identify the ideal weights for each asset. We will also improve your returns by lowering your taxes by tax-harvesting strategy. CGM will always provide the best investment allocation by keep improving the strategy.

The Set of Asset Classes

Why Use ETFs

An exchange-traded fund (ETF) is a security that tracks an index, a commodity or a basket of assets just like an index fund, but trades like a stock on an exchange. However, ETF has some advantages compared to stocks. One ETF can give exposure to a group of equities, market segments or styles. In comparison to a stock, the ETF can track a broader range of stocks, or even attempt to mimic the returns of a country or a group of countries. Addition, ETFs have much lower fees compared to other managed funds because of passively management. We choose ETFs in the consideration of their ranking and inception date.

Allocating Assets

CGM determines the optimal portfolio allocation by solving the Utility function using Mean-Variance Optimization. Each portfolio is created by calculating the portfolio weights to generate the maximum return under each risk tolerance level. MVO calculates the best risk-return trade-off when combining the asset classes in portfolios

Black-Litterman model

CGM use Black-Litterman as the key model in our trading strategy. Black-Litterman model is a mathematical model for portfolio allocation which aims to consider the investors’ “objective views” (i.e. the specific option about asset returns). For generate the objective views, we can use time series model (i.e. ARIMA model) and make it able to add some fundamental analysis which will be a great supplement to the original model to finally calculate the expected return for each asset class for optimizing the portfolio weights.

Risk Tolerance

Risk tolerance is a key consideration in determining your probable level of comfort with varying investing choices. Risk tolerance is your emotional capacity to withstand losses without panicking. Before the investing, you should have a realistic understanding of your risk tolerance: very conservative, conservative, moderate, aggressive or very aggressive. It is essential to measure your risk tolerance before investing so that we can find an asset allocation that reflects your risk tolerance.

For your convenience, we provide a questionnaire. click here On you finishing, we will automatically calculate the risk tolerance for your reference.

Tax Loss Harvesting

Tax loss harvesting is a strategy of selling securities at a loss to offset a capital gains tax liability. It is an efficient way to make an investment portfolio work even harder – not only generates investment returns but also generates tax savings. Be attention, tax loss harvesting is primarily a tax deferral strategy, and its benefit depends entirely on individual circumstances, through which you can defer tax liabilities, not avoid them. Capital losses can lower investor’s tax bill by offsetting gains, but the only way to realize a loss is to sell the depreciated asset.

Rebalancing & Ongoing Monitoring

The composition of any investment portfolio will naturally drift when the market price changes and a portfolio created by using MVO will not stay optimized over time as well. So CGM will always keep monitoring your portfolio and maintain the optimal weights. CGM will also optimize the best rebalancing frequency and the trade-off between transaction cost and portfolio drift. It is also important to note that the client can always change their investment goals and risk tolerance level any time when we are about to rebalance the portfolio.

Database

CGM uses MongoDB to store data in JSON-like documents which vary in structure. Embedded Data Models (“deformalized” models) are implemented and related information is stored together, which will give CMG fast query access and save your online waiting time.

Our database, RoboAdvisor, has four collections:

  1. Collection ETFs, which mainly stores the ETFs’ historical data and summary data; Data source includes Yahoo Finance and ETF.com
  2. Collection Views is used for Black-Litterman model and includes views from ARIMA model and views from Research Affiliates.
  3. Collection Test stores backtesting results and test parameters. It can be only accessed by portfolio managers.
  4. Collection Parameter stores a unique set of parameters which are used by every client.
